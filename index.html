How DriverPay Pro Works
A quick rundown of everything you can do.
Adding Loads

Type mileage values into the box at the top — comma-separated for multiple loads — and tap Add to Current Period. Each value becomes one load tagged with today's day.

To add loads to a past day or settlement, tap any settlement card to expand it, then hit + Add Load.

Tip: Settlements run Friday → Thursday and pay out the following Saturday. Anything you log auto-files into the right week.
Swipe Actions

Each load card responds to swipes:

← SWIPE RIGHT FOR EDIT
SWIPE LEFT FOR DELETE →

Edit lets you change tonnage, pricing, day, and add notes. Delete removes the load instantly.

Load Tickets

Every load has a camera icon. Red = no ticket uploaded yet. Blue = ticket on file. Tap the icon to capture, then tap again to view, retake, or delete.

Tip: The scanner auto-crops and enhances tickets so they read like a clean copy, even shot quickly with one hand.
Custom Pricing

The auto rate handles most loads, but for special jobs swipe-edit and pick a pricing mode:

Auto — standard rate table × tons × 30%.

Fixed $ — type the truck's total payout. You get 30% of that.

$ / Ton — type the truck's per-ton rate. You get 30% × rate × tons.

The driver pay preview updates live as you type. Loads with custom pricing show a blue · CUSTOM tag.

Notes

In the edit modal, drop a quick note on any load — broker name, ticket number, "wet load", whatever. A yellow note icon shows next to the camera so you know which loads have notes. Tap to read.

Revenue Charts

The three boxes at the bottom show This Month, This Year, and Lifetime earnings. Tap any one to see a bar chart breakdown — settlements within the month, months within the year, years across all time.

Settings

Open the menu (top right) → Settings to set your average tonnage anywhere from 17–30 tons. New loads default to this value. Existing loads keep their saved tonnage.

Your Data

All data lives locally on this device only — nothing is sent anywhere. Clearing browser data or switching devices wipes your history, so screenshot the revenue chart at year-end if you want a paper trail.
